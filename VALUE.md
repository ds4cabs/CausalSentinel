# Why CausalSentinel Matters

## The Problem
The single biggest driver of clinical-trial failure is choosing the wrong target. Roughly 90% of Phase II programs fail, and most failures trace back to a target-disease hypothesis that was correlational, not causal. Genetic causality — Mendelian randomization, colocalization, rare-variant burden — is the most reliable signal we have that intervening on a target will move the disease. But assembling that evidence is fragmented: GWAS catalogs, eQTL data, ClinVar, gnomAD, and PGx databases live in different places, in different formats.

## What CausalSentinel Does
Consolidates causal evidence into a single artifact for any (target, disease) pair. The output is a card with a causal verdict, the underlying MR / coloc evidence, population-constraint context, and the safety signals you'd want before betting a program on this target.

## Who Benefits
- Target-validation teams at biotechs
- Academic translational labs choosing candidates for grant submission
- VCs evaluating early-stage pitches
- Scientific advisors writing whitepapers

## Why Now
Open Targets v25+, OpenGWAS, UK Biobank cohorts, and high-quality MR pipelines have become accessible. LLMs can summarize the evidence narratively, in the form scientists actually need to read it.

## The Bigger Picture
Better target choice has compounding effects across the entire drug-development pipeline. A single avoided Phase III failure saves $200M+ and years of patient time. Modest improvements in causal-evidence rigor at the target-selection stage translate to industry-scale outcomes.
